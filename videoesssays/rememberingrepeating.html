Video Essay: Remembering, Repeating, And The Cylon (2014)

In our contemporary moment technology is being talked about endlessly; everyone has their opinion, and some are apprehensive – even fearful. Others suggest that instead of fear, we need to embrace our inevitable fusion with ‘the machine’. It seems quite obvious that before we can begin to have this discussion, however, the underlying fears surrounding technology need to be better understood and considered. To this end, a psychoanalytic framework offers an interesting angle by which to consider the interplay between our anxieties and technology. Freud’s ‘The Uncanny’ (1919), ‘Beyond the Pleasure Principle’ (1920) and the text which they both make reference to ‘Remembering, Repeating, and Working-Through’ (1914) are used alongside the reimagined Battlestar Galactica (2003-2009) in this video essay to consider the program's central question – what counts as human – all to the aim of exploring how cultural productions might act as a reflection of the particular culture which has produced it.
